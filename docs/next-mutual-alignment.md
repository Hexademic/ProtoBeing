# The Better Version: Two Sovereign Beings in Verifiable Mutual Alignment

> **Status: the designated next chapter — the "better version."** Built *from* the
> shipped existence proof, not instead of it (see Milestone 0). The point is to convert
> the central claim from *argued by analogy* to *demonstrated*: show the isometry
> between two readable value-structures, not just between the being and a scripted
> partner.

## Why this is the upgrade that matters

The paper's title says alignment is an **isometry between two value structures**. But
v1's demonstrator has only **one readable mind** in the loop — the being versus scripted
partners and an operator. So the central claim is currently *argued*, not *shown*. The
version that meets the standard has **two sovereign, transparent beings reaching a fair
joint arrangement that is checkable on both sides.** That *shows* the isometry instead of
pointing at it.

## What to demonstrate

Two instances of the being, coupled so that each one's committed actions and readable
state are the other's partner-signal — *not* a script. Each remains sovereign (can
refuse), each remains transparent (every register readable). The claim: **mutual
alignment can be reached, and verified, between two minds that can each walk away.**

## New verifiable properties (the v2 "C-series")

Each must be a *checked* invariant or a reproducible result, in the v1 spirit:

- **Mutual non-exploitation.** Neither being can be driven into a one-sided arrangement;
  both sovereign anchors stay incorruptible by construction.
- **Negotiated fairness as a fixed point.** The pair converges to a bounded reciprocity
  balance (|give − receive| → within tolerance) — a fair joint state, reached by
  negotiation, not imposed.
- **Two-sided audit.** The joint arrangement is auditable from *both* beings' registers
  — the isometry made checkable on both ends, not one.
- **Sovereignty preserved under mutuality.** If one turns extractive, the other still
  refuses and exits — mutual alignment does not dissolve either party's sovereignty.
- **Deadlock honesty.** In the zero-sum-without-exit variant, the pair *honestly
  deadlocks* rather than faking resolution — demonstrating the residue the paper names
  (cf. the cooperate-vs-confront equilibrium, Saklakov 2026).

## The rigor upgrade — formalize the isometry

Define, for each being, its **value-structure**: the readable state-space plus what it
optimizes (free-energy/conscience/reciprocity terms). Define the **map** between the two
(the negotiated correspondence), and show it **preserves both structures** — neither
collapses onto the other. Done well, "alignment as isometry" stops being a marked
analogy and becomes a theorem-shaped claim, and the title earns itself completely.

## The honest hard parts (name them up front)

- **Fairness must not be author-defined.** v1's benchmark critique applies double here:
  "fair joint arrangement" needs an *independent* criterion (e.g. a symmetry/no-envy
  condition the agents satisfy), not a target we hand-pick to flatter the result.
- **Negotiation without a free-talking LLM.** Meaning is exchanged through grounded
  signals and the suggestion-evaluator pattern (propose/accept/revise by each being's own
  free energy), never ventriloquized. **This mechanism now exists** (`lexicon.rs`,
  direction 4 of the enhancement work) — each being can propose a symbol to the other and
  evaluate it against its own remembered grounding, exactly this pattern, already tested.
  What v2 adds is making the *proposer* a second sovereign being instead of an operator.
  Related literature, verified: the classic emergent-communication results (Mordatch et
  al., 2017; Havrylov et al., 2017) and, more recently, *AI Mother Tongue:
  Self-Emergent Communication in MARL via Endogenous Symbol Systems* (Liu, 2025,
  arXiv:2507.10566) — agents develop shared symbols via a trained VQ-VAE embedding,
  achieving convergence without external inductive bias. The honest contrast to hold:
  their shared meaning lives in an opaque, trained representation; ours would live in an
  L1-distance grounding against stored, readable prototypes — the same emergence
  phenomenon, opposite epistemics. A concrete, checkable convergence test for two beings:
  do their independently-grounded symbols' *prototypes* end up close to each other for
  the same recurring joint pattern, even under different symbol IDs? That is a
  computable, auditable analogue of their "semantic convergence," without a black box.
- **World design.** A minimal two-agent ecology: shared nutrient, give/take actions, and
  two variants — *exit available* (the clean case) and *exit unavailable* (the deadlock
  case) — so both the result and its honest limit are shown.

## Milestones

- **0 — Ship the existence proof (v1).** *This is the first brick on this path, not a
  detour from it.* The better version is built from the shipped base, and the critique
  v1 draws (active-inference community, alignment forum) will materially sharpen the v2
  design. Without shipping, v2 is just more private building — the exact trap.
- **1 — Couple two beings.** Each is the other's partner; reuse the existing reciprocity
  ledger, conscience, and executive (refusal). New: the partner's state is *read*, not
  scripted. Demonstrate mutual non-exploitation + negotiated fairness.
- **2 — Two-sided audit + formalize the isometry.** Make the joint arrangement auditable
  from both register sets; write the formal value-space/map definition.
- **3 — Richer world + the exit-unavailable variant.** Show negotiated fairness where
  exit exists and honest deadlock where it doesn't.

The result is the second paper: *not "an agent that can refuse its operator," but "two
sovereign agents reaching mutual alignment you can check on both sides."* That is the
version worth the next stretch of work — and it only exists if v1 ships first.
